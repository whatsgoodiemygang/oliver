Guide

How to use the app.

Open the app at whatsgoodiemygang.github.io/oliver/ and click Sign In. Use your @abingdon.org.uk Google account for one-click login, or enter your school email and password manually.
Head to the Catalogue tab. Use the search bar to find books by title, author, or keyword. Filter by genre using the chips at the top. Click any book card to see its full details, availability, and reviews.
Go to the Research tab and type your question or topic in the chat box. The AI will suggest relevant books from the catalogue and provide reading guidance. Great for homework, project research, or just finding something new to read.
Visit the Discover tab and select how you're feeling from the mood board. The app will instantly surface a curated selection of books that match your vibe — adventurous, calm, curious, or anything in between.
The Challenge tab shows your current school reading challenge. Log books you've finished, track your progress bar, and earn milestones as you go. Challenges are set by library staff.
Open any book's detail page from the catalogue and scroll to the Reviews section. Give it a star rating, write your thoughts, and hit submit. Your review will appear instantly for other pupils to read.
Staff accounts automatically see the Admin tab after signing in. From here you can:
  • Add, edit, or remove books from the catalogue
  • Moderate and delete pupil reviews
  • Set and update the reading challenge
  • View pupil progress across the challenge
Deployment

Getting it running.

01

Fork or clone the repo

Go to github.com/whatsgoodiemygang/Oliver and fork the repo to your own account, or clone it locally.

02

Configure Firebase

Create a Firebase project at console.firebase.google.com. Enable Authentication (Google + Email/Password) and Firestore. Copy your config object into the app's Firebase config section.

03

Enable GitHub Pages

Go to your repo Settings → Pages, set the branch to main, and save. GitHub will publish your site automatically.

04

Add your domain to Firebase

In Firebase Console, go to Authentication → Settings → Authorised domains and add your GitHub Pages domain (e.g. whatsgoodiemygang.github.io).

05

Done

Visit your GitHub Pages URL and sign in. The app is live and fully functional — no server required.
